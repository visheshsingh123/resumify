Analytics & ad networks

No data ever uploaded

All form fields, your photo, and your generated resume are held in your browser's memory. Nothing is sent over the network — open your browser's dev tools and check for yourself.

No accounts, no tracking

There's nothing to sign up for and no email to give away. Resumify doesn't use cookies, analytics scripts, or third-party trackers of any kind.

Local-only storage

Your theme preference is the only thing saved, and it's stored in your browser's local storage — on your machine, under your control, and removable any time.

Open, inspectable code

Resumify is a single self-contained file. View source at any time to see exactly what runs — there's no hidden server-side logic to trust blindly.

Works fully offline

Once the page is loaded, you can disconnect from the internet entirely and keep building. No connection is required to fill in details or generate your PDF.

Close the tab, it's gone

Nothing persists beyond your session by default. Closing or refreshing the tab clears your data — ideal for shared or public computers.

FAQ

Common questions

Where is my data actually stored?

While you're editing, your information lives only in your browser tab's memory (JavaScript state). It is never written to a database, file server, or cloud storage. The only thing saved between visits is your light/dark theme preference, stored locally in your browser.

Does Resumify use any analytics, ads, or cookies?

No. There are no tracking pixels, no analytics SDKs, and no advertising scripts. The page makes no outbound network requests once it has loaded — you can verify this yourself in your browser's network inspector.

How do I get a PDF?

Use the Download PDF button, which opens your browser's native print dialog. Choose "Save as PDF" as the destination. The file is generated entirely on your device by your browser — Resumify never sees or handles the output file.

What happens to my photo if I upload one?

Your photo is read directly into the page using your browser's file APIs and displayed locally. It is never transmitted anywhere — it exists only as image data inside your current tab.

Is it really free?

Yes. There are no paid tiers, watermarks, or usage limits. Since there's no backend to run, there's nothing to charge for.
